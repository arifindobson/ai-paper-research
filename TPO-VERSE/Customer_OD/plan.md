## Key dataset stats (from `generate_figures.py`, seed 20250907)
- 232 land cells ≈ 171 km²; total population 1,610,885.
- Total captured revenue IDR 507.8 bn/month; served-cell ARPU ≈ IDR 361,662
  /person/month.
- Classification: 77 penetrated, 13 under-penetrated, 3 whitespace,
  139 low priority. Whitespace population 23,210 (1.4%); opportunity at
  served ARPU ≈ IDR 8.39 bn/month. Under-penetrated population 136,164.
- Gain curves: 50% of population in 25.4% of area (43 km²) via hex targeting
  vs 38.4% (66 km²) via whole districts; 75% → 47.0% (80 km²) vs 57.8% (99 km²).
- k-ring cannibalization demo: 1 shared cell of 19 per store (5%) at k≤2.

## Paper outline
1. **Introduction** — strategic market analysis across Indonesia is hostage to
   administrative geography; contributions list.
2. **Background** — H3 mechanics (hierarchy, aperture 7, resolutions,
   truncation), tooling ubiquity (warehouse-native SQL, ArcGIS Pro 3.1),
   retail-geography lineage (Reilly/Huff gravity, MAUP literature).
3. **The Case Against Administrative Geography** — MAUP, cross-border
   catchments, *pemekaran* instability (fig-maup).
4. **The H3 Spine Architecture** — ingestion→index→join→serve pipeline on the
   warehouse (TikZ pipeline diagram, drawn inline), governance.
5. **Analytical Method** — formal metrics layer on the spine: demand surface,
   revenue capture with distance decay (Huff-style), penetration
   classification rule, k-ring catchment/cannibalization, targeting gain
   curves. Notation table.
6. **Case Study: Western Jakarta–Tangerang Corridor** — synthetic corridor,
   walk through population → overlay → classification → scatter figures with
   the stats above; whitespace opportunity sizing.
7. **Targeting Efficiency & Field Applications** — gain/bars figures;
   logistics catchments, expansion planning, geofenced media, hypercare
   dispatch (from brief §3).
8. **Implementation Roadmap** — PoC → pipeline automation → national scale
   (brief §5), risks.
9. **Limitations & Future Work** — synthetic data caveat, ecological fallacy
   at coarse res, edge effects, integration with VRPTW-TERRA routing layer.
10. **Conclusion.**
11. **Bibliography** — Brodsky/H3, h3geo docs, Esri blog + Generate
    Tessellation doc, Openshaw (MAUP), Reilly, Huff, Zipf, Kontur population,
    Snowflake/BigQuery H3 docs, VRPTW-TERRA companion.
